\begin{example}
	Consider the following “sum of three cubes” benchmark\footnote{Benchmark \texttt{STC\_0079.smt2} from the
		\texttt{20220315-MathProblems} family in the SMT-LIB QF\_NIA suite.}:
	\begin{align*}
		x^3 + y^3 + z^3 = 79.
	\end{align*}
	Although there do exist specialized solvers\marek{cite techniques} for ``sum of three cubes'' problems, they still
	are posed as a challenge for contemporary SMT solvers to solve automatically, that is, without any specialized background theory
	of the said problem. However, none of \textsc{cvc5}, \textsc{mathsat}, \textsc{yices}
	or \textsc{z3} can solve it within a 3-minute time limit. Our solver can solve this example is \textsc{sat} in roughly 21
	seconds in its basic configuration, using only secant axioms. The model it finds is $x=-19, \,y=35, \,z=-33$.
	This is one of 231 ``sum of three cubes'' problems in the benchmark set our solver solves uniquely.
\end{example}

By inspection of Table~\ref{tab:secant-conds}, the two conditions satisfy the chains
\[
	\mathit{C}(x^k,v,\downarrow) \implies v^k \leq v^{k-1}x \leq x^k,
	\qquad
	\mathit{C}(x^k,v,\uparrow) \implies x^k \leq v^{k-1}x \leq v^k,
\]
so each condition simultaneously makes both $v^k$ and $v^{k-1}x$ lower (resp.\ upper) bounds on $x^k$,
with the linear bound $v^{k-1}x$ always the tighter of the two.

The validity of the lower-bound axioms (Table~\ref{tab:mul-axioms-lb}) follows from a two-step chain.
Under the conditions of any given row,
\[
	x^k y^l \;\geq\; x^k w^l \;\geq\; v^{k-1}x w^l.
\]

\emph{Step~1} (right inequality).
$\mathit{C}(x^k,v,{\cdot})$ bounds $x^k$ relative to $v^{k-1}x$.
Multiplying by $w^l$ preserves the direction when $w^l>0$ and reverses it when $w^l<0$.
The table compensates: it selects $\downarrow$ (i.e.\ $x^k \geq v^{k-1}x$) when $w^l>0$
and $\uparrow$ (i.e.\ $x^k \leq v^{k-1}x$) when $w^l<0$,
so that $x^k w^l \geq v^{k-1}x w^l$ in all four sign combinations.

\emph{Step~2} (left inequality).
$\mathit{C}(y^l,w,{\cdot})$ bounds $y^l$ relative to $w^l$.
Multiplying by $x^k$ preserves the direction when $x^k>0$ and reverses it when $x^k<0$.
Within the region defined by $\mathit{C}(x^k,v,{\cdot})$, the sign of $x^k$ matches the sign of $v^k$:
for even $k$ both are non-negative, and for odd $k$ the conditions in Table~\ref{tab:secant-conds}
confine $x$ and $v$ to the same side of zero.
The table selects $\downarrow$ (i.e.\ $y^l \geq w^l$) when $v^k>0$
and $\uparrow$ (i.e.\ $y^l \leq w^l$) when $v^k<0$,
so that $x^k y^l \geq x^k w^l$.

The upper-bound axioms (Table~\ref{tab:mul-axioms-ub}) follow from the symmetric chain
$x^k y^l \leq x^k w^l \leq v^{k-1}x w^l$,
with $\downarrow$ and $\uparrow$ swapped throughout. Note that similar axioms can be
constructed also by linearizing $y$ while fixing constant $x$.
